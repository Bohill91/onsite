# OnSite Rate Structure & Payments System

## Objective

Build OnSite’s rate and payment structure before adding market pricing data.

The system must:

* Let workers set a minimum acceptable day rate
* Let companies request labour
* Allow OnSite to set final pricing
* Keep OnSite margin hidden
* Never allow OnSite margin below 15%
* Generate weekly invoices from attendance
* Pay workers only after client funds are received
* Track company payment reliability

---

# 1. Worker Minimum Rate

During worker registration, add:

**Minimum Day Rate**

Example:

* £220/day
* £250/day
* £300/day

Rules:

* Worker must enter a minimum day rate
* OnSite must never offer work below this rate
* This rate is private
* Companies must not see the worker minimum rate

---

# 2. Company Job Request

When creating a job, company enters:

* Trade required
* Role / Grade
* Location
* Start date
* Duration
* Number of workers required
* Required qualifications
* Urgency
* Optional budget range

The company does not directly set the worker pay.

---

# 3. OnSite Pricing Calculation

For every booking, calculate:

## Worker Pay

Amount paid to worker.

## Company Charge Rate

All-in day rate charged to company.

## OnSite Margin

Company Charge Rate - Worker Pay = OnSite Margin

---

# 4. Minimum Margin Rule

OnSite margin must never go below:

**15% of worker pay**

Example:

Worker Pay:
£250/day

Minimum OnSite Margin:
£37.50/day

Minimum Company Charge:
£287.50/day

If company budget is too low, display:

**Budget too low to secure verified workers.**

---

# 5. Value Distribution Rule

When additional budget exists, distribute value in this order:

1. Increase worker pay
2. Increase OnSite margin
3. Keep company pricing attractive versus traditional recruiters

The objective is:

* Workers earn more
* OnSite earns healthy margin
* Companies still prefer OnSite over traditional recruiters

---

# 6. Visibility Rules

## Worker Sees

Only show:

* Job details
* Guaranteed pay rate
* Payment status

Do not show:

* Company charge rate
* OnSite margin

---

## Company Sees

Only show:

* Worker profile
* Compliance status
* Reliability score
* All-in day rate

Do not show:

* Worker minimum rate
* Worker pay
* OnSite margin

---

# 7. Weekly Attendance Cycle

Workers work Monday to Friday.

Attendance is recorded daily.

Statuses:

* On Time
* Late
* No Show
* Not Required Today
* Site Cancelled
* Under Review

---

# 8. Weekly Invoice Generation

Every Friday, generate company invoice from approved attendance records.

Invoice includes:

* Company name
* Job
* Site
* Worker count
* Days worked
* Charge rate
* Total amount due

Do not generate payment for:

* No Show
* Disputed attendance
* Attendance Under Review

---

# 9. Company Payment Terms

Default for new companies:

**Payment due within 3 working days of invoice**

Trusted companies:

**7-day terms**

Future admin-approved options:

* 14-day terms
* 30-day terms

Do not give 14 or 30 day terms automatically.

---

# 10. Worker Payment Rule

Workers are paid only when:

* Attendance is approved
* Company funds have been received
* No active dispute exists

OnSite must not fund payroll from its own cash reserves.

---

# 11. Worker Payment Statuses

Show workers:

* Earnings Pending
* Awaiting Client Payment
* Scheduled for Payment
* Paid
* On Hold Due to Dispute

This must be clear so workers understand payment depends on client funds being received.

---

# 12. Company Invoice Statuses

Show companies:

* Invoice Generated
* Payment Pending
* Payment Received
* Overdue
* Account Restricted

---

# 13. Company Payment Reliability Score

Every company should have a payment reliability score.

Track:

* Number of invoices issued
* Number of invoices paid
* Average days to pay
* Number of overdue invoices
* Total overdue amount

Example display:

Payment Reliability:
98%

Average Payment Time:
2.3 days

---

# 14. Automatic Restrictions

If a company has an overdue invoice:

Automatically:

* Block new job posts
* Block new bookings
* Notify admin
* Mark account as restricted

If overdue beyond admin-set limit:

* Suspend company account

---

# 15. Admin Controls

Admin can:

* View invoices
* Mark invoice as paid
* Adjust payment status
* Set company payment terms
* Restrict company account
* Suspend company account
* View worker payment statuses
* Release worker payments after client funds clear

---

# 16. Payment Provider Preparation

Build architecture so Stripe can be added later.

Prepare for:

* Stripe invoices
* Stripe payment links
* Stripe Connect worker payouts
* Webhooks for payment success/failure

But for now, allow manual admin confirmation of payments received into the business bank account.

---

# Core Rule

OnSite should never release worker payments unless client funds have been received.

The platform must protect cashflow while still making payments transparent and reliable for workers.
