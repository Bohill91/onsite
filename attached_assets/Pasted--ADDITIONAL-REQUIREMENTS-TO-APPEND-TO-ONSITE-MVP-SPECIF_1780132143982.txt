# Worker Registration Flow

Worker registration should be broken into stages to maximise sign-up completion.

---

## Step 1: Basic Details

Fields:

* Full Name
* Email Address
* Mobile Number
* Password

---

## Step 2: Trade Information

Fields:

* Trade
* Role / Grade
* Years Experience
* Location
* Minimum Daily Rate

Examples:

Electrical:

* Mate
* Improver
* Electrician
* Approved Electrician
* Supervisor

Plumbing:

* Mate
* Improver
* Plumber
* Pipefitter
* Mechanical Supervisor

Carpentry:

* Mate
* Carpenter
* Shopfitter
* Supervisor

Groundworks:

* Labourer
* Groundworker
* Plant Operator
* Foreman

The system should support adding additional trades and grades from the admin panel.

---

## Step 3: Compliance Verification

Workers must complete compliance verification before becoming eligible for work.

Required documents:

### UTR Number

* 10-digit UTR number field
* Validation required

### Right to Work in the UK

Accepted documents:

* British Passport
* Irish Passport
* UK Share Code
* Biometric Residence Permit

### Photo Identification

Accepted documents:

* Passport
* Driving Licence

### Trade Cards & Certifications

Examples:

* CSCS
* ECS
* JIB
* IPAF
* PASMA
* SSSTS
* SMSTS
* First Aid

Allow multiple document uploads.

---

# Worker Verification Statuses

Workers should have one of the following statuses:

### Incomplete Registration

Profile created.

Cannot:

* Apply for jobs
* Accept jobs
* Be matched automatically

---

### Pending Verification

Documents uploaded.

Awaiting admin review.

Cannot:

* Accept jobs

---

### Verified

All compliance checks approved.

Can:

* Apply for jobs
* Accept jobs
* Be automatically matched

Only verified workers should appear in search results and auto-match algorithms.

---

# Worker Profile Completion System

Display profile completion percentage.

Examples:

Basic Information ✔

Trade Information ✔

Profile Photo ✔

UTR Number ✔

Right to Work ✔

Qualifications ✔

Completion:

The platform should encourage workers to reach 100% completion.

Profiles with higher completion should rank higher in search and matching.

# Attendance System (Updated Requirements)

Attendance is one of the platform's core features.

Workers MUST NOT self-report reliability.

Reliability must be generated from actual attendance records.

---

## Default Reliability Score

Every worker starts with:

Reliability Score = 100%

No worker can manually edit or influence this score.

---

## Daily Attendance Submission

The client/site manager must record attendance daily.

Attendance options:

* On Time
* Late
* No Show
* Not Required Today
* Site Cancelled

Attendance should be fast and mobile-friendly.

Target completion time:

Less than 30 seconds for an entire workforce.

---

## Reliability Tracking

Reliability should be calculated automatically.

Examples:

On Time:
No penalty

Late:
Small penalty

No Show:
Major penalty

Client Cancelled:
No impact

Site Closed:
No impact

---

## Performance Metrics

Track three independent scores:

### Reliability

Did they attend?

### Punctuality

Did they arrive on time?

### Performance

How did the client rate their work?

---

## Example Worker Statistics

Reliability:
99.4%

Punctuality:
96.7%

Performance:
4.8/5

Completed Shifts:
142

No Shows:
1

Late Arrivals:
6

These statistics should become a key differentiator for OnSite.

---

# Attendance-Driven Payroll & Invoicing

Attendance submissions should automatically generate:

* Approved Timesheets
* Payroll Records
* Invoices

Goal:

Eliminate manual timesheets and recruiter administration.

---

# Attendance Disputes

Workers must be able to challenge attendance records.

Example:

Client marks No Show.

Worker submits dispute.

Admin reviews evidence.

Admin can amend attendance records if required.

This prevents abuse and protects worker reliability scores.

---

# Future Payroll Features

Prepare database architecture for:

* CIS payments
* PAYE workers
* Payroll exports
* Invoice generation
* Accounting software integrations

Even if these features are not included in MVP, the architecture should support future implementation.

---

# Core Principle

OnSite should become the trusted workforce operating system for construction.

Trust should be built through:

* Verified workers
* Compliance tracking
* Attendance records
* Reliability scoring
* Automated matching
* Automated payroll approval
* Automated workforce management

The long-term objective is to replace traditional recruitment administration with technology and performance data.
